WHISKEY TOWN

San Francisco, California, during the Gold Rush, was America’s quintessential “Whiskey Town”. Its notorious Barbary Coast had crime, depravity, and viciousness, yet possessed more glamour than any other area of vice and iniquity on the American continent. San Francisco’s growth was driven by the influx of gold seekers, the “49er’s”, and the hordes of gamblers, thieves, harlots, politicians and other felonious parasites who tagged along and developed a taste for fine food, spirits, and adventuring.

We are developing a developing a range of whiskeys for Whiskey Town (TM) and as we were waiting for them to be ready we discovered a few lost and forgotten barrels in Kentucky. Our first Lost Barrel series offering is an extremely rare 26-year American Whiskey. This incredibly special Whiskey has a subtle and smooth flavor that comes with its age as well as a mysterious history. We don’t know the entire story because the Lost Barrels came to us under a non-disclosure agreement, but this is what we do know: The whiskey was originally mashed, fermented and distilled in Indiana. It was a special, very limited, experimental run which never reached the market. While we don’t know the ultimate mash bill it has a high corn percentage. The barrels were then transferred to Kentucky and lost, only to be discovered 26-years later. There are only a few of these barrels in existence and we got our hands on them. Considered this a one of a kind American Whiskey, aged 26 years, and bottled at cask strength.

To paraphrase Fred Minnick, “It hard to underestimate the importance of Rum. In the last three centuries, Rum has been at the center of commerce, nation building, wars at sea, wars in court. No other spirit can tell so many tales of triumph and tragedy.”

We love Rum because of its maritime history, centered around the New World and navies and what it means to us when we mix it with ginger beer, tonic, fruit juice or just sip it straight.
